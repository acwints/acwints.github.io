I'm almost positive I consume way more information and media than you're supposed to, so the only way I can justify it is to share the best things I've found, partially just so I have a record of where my time went, but also so other people can benefit from my addiction. This will include Articles, Podcasts, TV Shows, Books, Products and anything else I may have stumbled across that resonated with me.

March 2021

Podcast
Whoop: Will Ahmed

I still don’t own a Whoop but I’m getting pretty close. This podcast was actually on a different network and interviews Whoop’s founder, Will Ahmed, who is not surprisingly a pretty impressive dude. He’s also been super candid in how he’s approached building a company at such a young age so it’s cool to hear those insights from him.

February 2021

Article
A Private Stock Exchange

I’m pretty interested in the evolution of the private markets, and in providing startup employees a way to get out of the bind that many find themselves in. In the venture world specifically, founders and executives are typically able to cash out a sizable portion of their equity well before any type of public exit, yet it’s almost taboo to talk about it because the messaging to employees still needs to be that everyone has skin in the game. And I don’t blame those individuals at all, but it’s certainly past time for employees to be able to access similar liquidation options, and CartaX appears to be making that a reality.

Article
Consumer Subscriptions

After 4 years at ClassPass, Consumer Subscriptions is a domain I can actually feel confident discussing in depth. Like Nikhil, I agree that subscription fatigue is likely overblown, as recurring charges are often simply a better, more elegant payment structure to give consumers what they already want. That said, I think there are some companies offering subscriptions where you have a square peg trying to fit in a round hole, but recurring revenue is such a buzzword for investors that it's hard to blame them.
